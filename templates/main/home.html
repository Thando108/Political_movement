About Us

Our Philosophy

At the core of our movement is the devotion (bhakti) to God (Krishna). We hold that pure, loving devotion to Krishna is the highest goal of life, bringing profound joy and fulfillment. This practice, known as sankirtan, purifies the heart and draws us closer to the divine.

What We Do

Congregational Chanting (Sankirtan): We emphasize the communal chanting of the holy names of God, particularly the Hare Krishna mantra: “Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna Krishna, Hare Hare / Hare Rama, Hare Rama, Rama Rama, Hare Hare.”

Teachings of Lord Caitanya

Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu, revered as an incarnation of Krishna, devoted his life to spreading the practice of sankirtan and teaching the principles of love and devotion. His life and teachings are preserved in sacred texts such as the Chaitanya Charitamrita and Chaitanya Bhagavata.

Our Activities

We gather in public spaces to sing and chant the Hare Krishna mantra, creating an uplifting and joyous atmosphere. We also celebrate various festivals. Our followers engage in the study of sacred texts like the Bhagavad Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam, participating in discussions and lectures to deepen their spiritual understanding.

Global Influence

The Sankirtan Movement has a profound global impact, thanks largely to the efforts of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON). ISKCON has established temples and communities worldwide, spreading the message of Lord Caitanya and the practice of sankirtan.
